# Monthly Review Logic

Operational pressure rarely appears suddenly.

Most agencies experience structural drift gradually.

This framework uses monthly review cadence to detect operational pressure before it becomes financially disruptive.

## Why Monthly Reviews Matter

Weekly optimization often creates noise.

Short-term fluctuations can hide deeper structural patterns.

Monthly reviews create enough distance to evaluate:

- contribution trends
- client pressure
- delivery expansion
- hiring timing
- operational stability

## Review Philosophy

The goal is not reacting faster.

The goal is reacting more clearly.

A structured monthly cadence helps founders avoid emotional operational decisions.

## Typical Review Signals

Examples of signals reviewed monthly:

- contribution compression
- unstable delivery load
- margin inconsistency
- client concentration risk
- hiring pressure
- operational expansion without pricing adjustment

## Decision Perspective

Growth should remain economically visible.

When operational complexity grows faster than visibility, decision quality declines.
